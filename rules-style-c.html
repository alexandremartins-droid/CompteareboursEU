Stellantis Informations

Article 2: Definitions

In these rules, each of the expressions below will have the meaning specified in its definition:

  • "PARTICIPANT": Person who participates in the GAME by meeting the participation conditions
  • "CHALLENGE": Multiple-choice questionnaire or open questions, etc. to which Participants must correctly respond in full to win this game
  • "SHAREPOINT": Platform on which the game is hosted for each of the participating regions.
Article 3: Preamble

The "Mission: Connected Heroes" Challenge is a unifying global initiative that aims to bring together more than 22,000 STELLANTIS employees from the PDT - Product Development and Technology entity. This challenge, deployed simultaneously in four major strategic regions (North America, Europe, South America, and IAP/China), aims to create a common culture and harmonize knowledge around connected services.

Article 4: Challenge Objectives

The "Mission: Connected Heroes" Challenge pursues four main objectives:

  • Unite employees from the 4 regions around a common, engaging, and unprecedented program
  • Clarify and give meaning to the group's connected services offering
  • Train the best ambassadors capable of promoting connected services
  • Engage teams by strengthening pride and belonging through a fun and digital experience
Article 5: Participation Conditions

5.1 Eligibility

The challenge is exclusively reserved for internal STELLANTIS employees working under the PDT - Product Development and Technology entity (=PARTICIPANT), provided they have a permanent contract in these services from the beginning to the end of the challenge. Employees joining the company during the program will not be able to participate, from December 8th.

Only employees with permanent contracts are eligible; external service providers are explicitly excluded from this competition.

The Organizing Company reserves the right to add participants from outside the PDT entity.

5.2 Language

The official language of this challenge is English.

5.3 Participation

Participation is strictly voluntary. Only one participation per person is allowed (no team). Access to the challenge portal is only via the SharePoint platform. Connection is made with the STELLANTIS username and password.

The Organizing Company can unilaterally terminate the Participant's participation, without notice or justification, and without this being able to form any claim against it by the Participant, particularly if the information provided is contrary to the rules of participation.

The Participant can end their participation at any time by writing to the Organizing Company to delete their data.

Article 6: Personal Data Protection

6.1 Data collected

As part of the "Mission: Connected Heroes" Challenge, STELLANTIS, as the data controller, collects and processes certain personal data of Participants, including:

  • First and last name
  • Professional email address
  • STELLANTIS identifier
  • Entity/department
  • Region and country of assignment
  • Connection and participation data (dates, times, scores, responses)
  • Content created as part of the Challenge

6.2 Purposes of processing

The personal data of Participants are collected and processed for the exclusive purposes of:

  • Managing participation in the Challenge and its smooth running
  • Determining winners and awarding prizes
  • Anonymized statistical analysis of performance by region/country
  • Internal communication relating to the Challenge and its results
  • Improving knowledge of connected services within the Group

6.3 Legal basis

The processing of Participants' personal data is based on:

  • The execution of these rules, considered as a contract between STELLANTIS and the Participant
  • STELLANTIS's legitimate interest in promoting knowledge of connected services
  • The Participant's consent for specific processing, particularly the internal distribution of created content

6.4 Retention period

Participants' personal data are kept for the duration of the Challenge and up to three (3) months after the prize-giving, to allow for the processing of any claims. At the end of this period, the data will either be deleted or anonymized for statistical purposes and overall performance analysis.

6.5 Recipients of data

The personal data collected are intended for:

  • The organizing teams of the Challenge within STELLANTIS
  • Technical service providers mandated for the management of the SharePoint platform
  • The bailiff in charge of supervising the Challenge, only for the data necessary for their mission
  • Internal communication services for winners who have consented to the publication of their identity

No personal data will be transferred outside the European Economic Area without implementing appropriate safeguards in accordance with the GDPR.

  • A right to data portability

Article 8: Challenge Journey

The challenge journey is divided into four progressive phases, each designed to develop participants' knowledge and skills around STELLANTIS connected services:

8.1 Educational Phase (THE ACADEMY)

This first fundamental mission takes place in three distinct sessions, each lasting between 10 and 15 minutes. It aims to build a solid foundation of knowledge about connected services:

  • #1: Discovery of STELLANTIS connected services fundamentals via the Drive Smarter session
  • #2: Discovery of STELLANTIS connected services fundamentals via the Life Easier session
  • #3: Discovery of STELLANTIS connected services fundamentals via the Trip Enjoyable session

8.2 Practical Phase (THE PRACTICE)

In this second phase, the participant is placed in a simulation of a long-distance logistics journey, presented as an interactive course. At different stages of the virtual journey, the participant is confronted with situations requiring decision-making that will determine the participant's subsequent journey. For each situation, several response options are offered to the participant. No answer is considered incorrect, but some choices may be more efficient than others in terms of time, resources, or journey optimization.

8.3 Prospective Brainstorming Phase (THE LABORATORY)

This third phase is a creative test aimed at imagining the connected services of the future. The participant is invited to answer an open question about potential innovations in the field of connected logistics services. The response must be formulated in text form in the dedicated space, in English, and without character limit.

Each of these three sessions allows participants to accumulate essential points for qualification to the final phase, while consolidating their expertise and knowledge.

8.4 Finale (THE BIG FINALE)

The grand finale consists of creating original content showcasing and highlighting one or more connected services, from an internal communication perspective. Finalists can submit their creation in one of the following formats:

  • Video content (maximum duration: 2 minutes)
  • Audio content (maximum duration: 3 minutes)
  • Detailed scenario (Word, PDF)
  • Visual or infographic (JPG, PNG, PDF)

Only employees who have obtained the highest number of points at the end of the weekly challenges are qualified to participate in the grand finale, according to the following regional distribution:

  • Top 200 in Europe
  • Top 100 in North America
  • Top 35 in South America
  • Top 35 in the IAP/China area

At the end of the creation period, all challenge participants, whether or not they participated in the Grand Finale, are invited to vote for the finalist content. Each voter has multiple votes but only one vote per content. The winners of the finale are determined exclusively by the number of votes received. In case of a perfect tie between several finalists, previous points and then completion time will be used as tie-breakers; if still tied, a draw under bailiff control will determine the winner.

Article 9: Point System

9.1 Basic rules

MISSION #1

  • Mandatory and sometimes multiple answers
  • Minimum score: 0 (no negative score, it remains at 0)
  • Correct answer: 1 point added
  • Incorrect answer: 1 point deducted

Bonus: this challenge will be timed, and the fastest participants will earn higher places in the overall regional rankings.

MISSION #2

A score is awarded based on the effectiveness of the choices made (from 1 to 3 points at each stage).

MISSION #3

  • Answer provided: 1 point added
  • Answer not provided: 1 point deducted

GRAND FINALE

The final ranking of winners is based primarily on the number of votes received during the final phase. In case of a perfect tie between several finalists, the cumulative points obtained during the previous phases will be examined, designating as the winner the participant who demonstrated the best overall performance. If the tie persists, the time factor will come into play. If still identical, a draw supervised by a bailiff will determine the winner.

9.3 Rankings

At each stage, a ranking by region and country will be available, as well as the ranking of the connected person. The ranking is done by first name + 1st letter of the last name.
