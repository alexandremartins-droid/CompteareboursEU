Article 2: Game Duration and Global Description

The Game will take place from 8 December 2025 to 14 March 2026 inclusive. The Game will take place from 8 December 2025 to 14 March 2026 included.

The Game entitled “Mission: Connected Heroes” is a unifying global initiative that aims to bring together more than 22,000 STELLANTIS employees from the Product Development and Technology Business Unit (hereafter referred as “PDT”). This challenge, deployed simultaneously in four major strategic regions (North America, Europe, South America, and IAP/China), aims to create a common culture and harmonize knowledge around connected services.

In these rules, each of the expressions below will have the meaning specified in its definition:

  • "Sharepoint": Platform on which the Game is hosted for each of the participating regions.
Article 4: Challenge Objectives

The Game pursues four main objectives:

  • Unite participants from the 4 regions around a common, engaging, and unprecedented program

Article 8: Game Journey

The Game journey is divided into four progressive phases, each designed to develop participants' knowledge and skills around STELLANTIS connected services:

8.1 Educational Phase (THE ACADEMY)

This first fundamental mission takes place in three distinct sessions, each lasting between 10 and 15 minutes. It aims to build a solid foundation of knowledge of connected services:

  • #1: Discovery of STELLANTIS connected service fundamentals via the Drive Smarter session
  • #2: Discovery of STELLANTIS connected service fundamentals via the Easier Life session
  • #3: Discovery of STELLANTIS connected service fundamentals via the Enjoyable Trip session

8.2 Practical Phase (THE PRACTICE EXCURSION)

In this second phase, the participant is placed in a simulation of a long-distance logistics journey, presented as an interactive course. At different stages of the virtual journey, the participant is confronted with situations requiring decision-making that will determine the participant's subsequent journey. For each situation, several response options are offered to the participant. No answer is considered incorrect, but some choices may be more efficient than others in terms of time, resources, or journey optimization.

8.3 Prospective Brainstorming Phase (THE LABORATORY)

This third phase is a creative test aimed at imagining the connected services of the future. The participant is invited to answer an open question about potential innovations in the field of connected logistics services. The response must be formulated in text form in the dedicated space, in English, and without character limit.

Each of these three sessions allows participants to accumulate essential points for qualification in the final phase, while consolidating their expertise and knowledge.

8.4 Finale (THE BIG FINALE)

The grand finale consists of creating original content showcasing and highlighting one or more connected services, from an internal communication perspective. Finalists can submit their creation in one of the following formats:

  • Video content (maximum duration: 2 minutes)
  • Audio content (maximum duration: 3 minutes)
  • Detailed scenario (Word, PDF)
  • Visual or infographic (accepted formats: JPG, PNG, PDF)

For the development of their creation, finalists shall exclusively use STLA Artificial Intelligence tools.

Only employees who have obtained the highest number of points at the end of the five weekly challenges are qualified to participate in the grand finale, with the following regional distribution:

  • Top 200 in Europe
  • Top 100 in North America
  • Top 35 in South America
  • Top 35 in the IAP/China area

At the end of the creation period, all challenge participants, irrespective of whether they participated in the Grand Finale, are invited to vote for the finalist content. Each voter has multiple votes but only one vote per content. You cannot vote multiple times for the same content.

The winners of the finale are determined exclusively by the number of votes received. In case of a perfect tie between several finalists, the Organizing Company will look at their points from the previous challenge missions. The finalist who had the most points will win the allocated prize.

If the number of points is similar, the finalist who responded to the different missions in the least time will win the tiebreaker. If there is perfect similarity in points and time, we plan a draw supervised by a bailiff to determine the winner: Maître Doniol, 7 rue Jean Jaurès- 77410 Claye Souilly

Article 9: Points System

9.1 Basic rules

MISSION #1

  • Mandatory and sometimes multiple answers
  • Minimum score: 0 (no negative score, it remains at 0)
  • Correct answer: 1 point added
  • Incorrect answer: 1 point deducted

Bonus: this challenge will be timed, and the fastest participants will earn higher places in the overall regional rankings.

MISSION #2

  • A score is awarded based on the effectiveness of the choices made (from 1 to 3 points at each stage).
  • A bonus of 2 points will be awarded if the mission is completed successfully and on time. A bonus of 1 point will be awarded if the mission is completed successfully but later. No points will be awarded for failure.
  • For the mission concerning Stellantis Group connected vehicle users: 1 point is awarded if all fields in the open-ended questions are completed. 0 points otherwise.
  • For the mission concerning non-vehicle users or non-users of Stellantis Group connected vehicles: 1 point is awarded if all fields in the open-ended questions are completed. 0 points otherwise.

MISSION #3

  • Answer provided: 1 point added
  • Answer not provided: 0 points added

9.2 Grand Finale

The final ranking of winners is based primarily on the number of votes received during the final phase. In the event of a perfect tie between several finalists, a progressive tiebreaker system applies on the following basis:

  • The cumulative points obtained during the previous phases will be examined, designating as the winner the participant who demonstrated the best overall performance.
  • If the tie persists, the time factor will come into play: the participant who completed all the missions with the greatest speed will win the advantage.
  • In the exceptional event of a perfect equality in both points and completion time, a draw supervised by a bailiff will determine the final winner.

This rigorous methodology guarantees the fair designation of participants who will benefit from the rewards offered by STELLANTIS, detailed below.
